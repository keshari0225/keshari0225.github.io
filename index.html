Keshari Nandan

I'm a Postdoctoral Associate at Electrical and Computer Engineering, University of Minnesota, Minneapolis, Minnesota, where I mostly work on Cryogenic Computing on Silicon CMOS Plateform and Physics-Embedded Machine-learning for Semiconductor Device Modeling.

I graduated from IIT Kanpur, Kanpur, where I was advised by Yogesh S. Chauhan and Somnath Bhowmick. At IIT Kanpur, my research primarily focused on advanced carrier transport in silicon and low-dimensional channel material–based FETs, including the development of compact models and benchmarking for advanced FET and exploratory device concepts.

My research covers a broad spectrum, ranging from the core principles to practical implementations in electron devices and materials.

Email: knandan@umn.edu

Broad Area of Research

  • Scientific Machine Learning for Microelectronics

I'm interested in Low Temperature Logic and Memory Technology, Machine-learning for Nanoelectronics, and Developement and Application of Ab initio based tools for Exploration of Next-generation Materials and Devices. Most of my research centers on the intersection of materials and devices, aiming to advance energy-efficient logic and memory systems. It’s a fusion of cutting-edge futuristic concepts with practical applications in the present day.
