Course Notes: Introduction to Semiconductor Physics

Topic 1: Basics of Semiconductors

Semiconductors are materials with electrical conductivity between conductors and insulators. Key properties include:

  • Bandgap energy
  • Charge carrier mobility
  • Temperature-dependent conductivity
Additional Notes:

At room temperature, intrinsic semiconductors exhibit low conductivity, which can be enhanced by doping.

Topic 2: Types of Semiconductors

Semiconductors can be categorized into:

  • Intrinsic Semiconductors
  • Extrinsic Semiconductors (n-type, p-type)
Additional Notes:

Extrinsic semiconductors are doped to introduce free carriers for enhanced conductivity.
